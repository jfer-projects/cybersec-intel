# Stakeholder Mapping: Cybersecurity Intel Hub
**Phase 2 Prerequisite**  
**Timeline:** May 15-20, 2026  
**Owner:** Johnathan  
**Output:** Stakeholder needs document → Informs Phase 2 feature design

---

## Purpose
Conduct structured interviews with key stakeholders to clarify:
- Who uses the intel and how frequently
- What specific decisions they make with it
- What information gaps exist today
- Confidentiality and access control concerns
- Feature priorities and success criteria

---

## Stakeholder Groups & Interview Guide

### Group 1: Executive Leadership
**Target:** 1-2 executives (CEO, VP of Product, VP of Strategy)  
**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Key Questions:**

1. **Current Usage**
   - How do you currently use competitive and threat intelligence in strategic planning?
   - What formats work best for you (weekly briefs, quarterly summaries, interactive dashboards)?
   - How much time can you dedicate to reviewing intel per week/month?

2. **Decision-Making**
   - What are your top 3 strategic questions this year? (Market position, roadmap priorities, M&A opportunities, etc.)
   - Which competitive or threat signals would directly influence a major decision?
   - Example: "If we knew XYZ competitor was launching a bundled MSP offering, would it change our Q3 roadmap?"

3. **Information Gaps**
   - What do you wish you knew about competitors or threats that you don't have visibility into today?
   - Are there any blind spots in our current coverage? (Specific competitors, threat categories, customer segments)
   - Do you need more context on *why* findings matter to our business?

4. **Format & Cadence**
   - Would a monthly executive summary (3-5 pages) be useful? Quarterly?
   - What level of detail: High-level trends only, or drill-down detail available on demand?
   - Should it include roadmap implications (e.g., "This threat suggests we prioritize X in Q3")?

5. **Board-Level Readiness**
   - Do you need intel packaged for board/investor conversations?
   - What proof points or differentiation narratives would strengthen our positioning story?

---

### Group 2: Product Management Team
**Target:** 1-2 product managers  
**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Key Questions:**

1. **Current Usage**
   - How do you currently use competitive and threat intelligence in roadmap prioritization?
   - Which findings have influenced past feature decisions? (Get 1-2 concrete examples)
   - How do you currently discover which threats customers care about most?

2. **Decision-Making**
   - When deciding whether to build a feature, what competitive and threat context do you need?
   - Example question you'd want answered: "Are competitors moving toward X? Should we build it or accept parity?"
   - How do you currently validate that we're solving threats your customers actually face?

3. **Information Gaps**
   - What's missing from current competitive briefings that would help you prioritize better?
   - Do you need threat-to-feature mapping? (This threat is active → Our product addresses it via [feature])
   - Which competitors should we track more deeply per product category?

4. **Format & Output**
   - Would a per-category view work? (EDR competitive moves, Ransomware threat trends, etc.)
   - Should threat briefings include "product implications" sections?
   - Do you want raw findings, or curated insights with recommendations?

5. **Integration with Planning**
   - How could intel feed directly into sprint planning or quarterly planning cycles?
   - Would a "threat-to-roadmap alignment" checklist be useful?

---

### Group 3: Sales & Channel
**Target:** 2 sales leaders or top reps + 1 channel partner manager  
**Duration:** 30 minutes  
**Key Questions:**

1. **Current Usage**
   - How do you currently use competitive and threat intelligence in customer conversations?
   - Do you have battle cards? How are they created and kept fresh?
   - How often do customers ask about threats? Competitor features?

2. **Deal Implications**
   - Tell me about a recent lost deal. What role did competitive or threat factors play?
   - What competitive or threat info would have helped you win that deal?
   - Are there themes in what you lose on? (Price, features, threat response speed, etc.)

3. **Information Gaps**
   - What competitor moves surprised you (in a bad way)? Why weren't you prepared?
   - Which threats do MSB/SMB customers worry about most? Are we tracking those?
   - Do you need faster updates on competitive moves? (Weekly, daily?)

4. **Format & Output**
   - Battle cards: What format works? (1-pager? Interactive? Tied to specific threats?)
   - Would threat briefings with "customer buying triggers" help? (E.g., "LockBit ransomware spike → customers shopping for EDR")
   - Should intel be tied to account lists? (E.g., "Competitor X just moved in your territory")

5. **Confidentiality**
   - What intel is safe to share with channel partners? What needs to stay internal?
   - Are there deals in flight that need intel support right now?

---

### Group 4: Support & Operations
**Target:** 1 support manager or technical account manager  
**Duration:** 20 minutes  
**Key Questions:**

1. **Current Usage**
   - How do you use competitive and threat intelligence today?
   - Do customers bring threats or competitive concerns to support tickets?
   - Are there patterns in what customers ask about?

2. **Churn & Retention Signals**
   - Have we lost customers due to competitive moves or unaddressed threats?
   - What would help you identify churn risk earlier?
   - Would "threat-to-feature" mapping help you explain our roadmap to at-risk customers?

3. **Win/Loss Insights**
   - Can you contribute lost deal or churn feedback to a monthly synthesis process?
   - What format works best for sharing that feedback? (Quick form, Slack updates, monthly meeting)

## Synthesis Process
After all interviews complete (May 20):

1. **Consolidate findings** by stakeholder group (Execs, PMs, Sales, Support)
2. **Identify common themes** (information gaps, format preferences, integration points)
3. **Spot conflicts** (e.g., Execs want monthly, Sales want daily)
4. **Recommend Phase 2 priorities** based on:
   - Frequency of request (How many stakeholders asked for this?)
   - Decisional impact (Does this influence major decisions?)
   - Effort to deliver (Can we build this quickly?)
5. **Create Phase 2 feature roadmap** prioritized by impact and effort
