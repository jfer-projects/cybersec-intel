🎯 Top 3 This Quarter

🟢 1. Ransomware Evolution — New Attack Methods
Shift from encryption to encryptionless extortion; post-quantum cryptography adoption. All endpoint + MDR products need response capability updates.
Impact: Action Required for Endpoint, EDR, MDR roadmaps
▼
Why This Matters: Ransomware attack methods are evolving faster than our detection capabilities. The shift to encryptionless extortion makes behavioral analysis critical; post-quantum adoption affects encryption-detection logic.
What's Happening: LockBit, BlackCat, and emerging groups now focus on data exfiltration + pressure (without encryption). This bypasses traditional "detect encryption" signatures. Post-quantum cryptography in some variants complicates detection/response.
Portfolio Impact:
  • 🔴 Endpoint Protection: Need to update signatures, add behavioral detection for exfiltration patterns
  • 🔴 EDR: Behavioral analysis of file access + network I/O critical; response playbooks need updating
  • 🔴 MDR: SOC needs playbooks for non-encryption ransomware (what to look for?)
Recommended Action: Q2 2026 roadmap: Add exfiltration detection to EDR/MDR. Threat Intel: create guide for "ransomware without encryption" detection patterns.
Sources: CISA RomCom reports (Q1 2026), LockBit variant analysis (BleepingComputer May 2026), CrowdStrike threat reports
🟡 2. Arctic Wolf Gartner Win — Customer Validation
4.9/5.0 rating; 99% NPS validates MDR market strength. Competitive positioning threat against our MDR roadmap decisions.
Impact: Parity check needed for MDR feature differentiation
▼
Why This Matters: Arctic Wolf's Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader status (4.9/5.0) signals to buyers that managed detection is table stakes. Their 99% NPS suggests execution excellence; this raises customer expectations across the entire MDR category.
What's Happening: Arctic Wolf published Gartner positioning in Q4 2025. Their focus on MSP/SMB ease-of-use + 24/7 SOC is resonating. Customer testimonials emphasize simplicity and "set-and-forget" operations. Buyer confidence in MDR is at an all-time high.
Portfolio Impact:
  • 🟡 MDR Roadmap: Customers now expect Gartner-leader-level ease of use. Our current roadmap may look feature-poor vs. Arctic Wolf's simplicity.
  • 🟡 Sales: Lost deals mentioning "Arctic Wolf's ease of use" or "99% NPS" are now more common (validation signal).
  • 🟡 Product Team: Need to clarify: Do we differentiate on ease-of-use, feature richness, or something else?
Recommended Action: (1) Audit our MDR against Arctic Wolf features/UX. (2) Define our differentiation. (3) Sales: Prepare Arctic Wolf comparison docs for competitive conversations.
Sources: Gartner Magic Quadrant for SIEM (Q4 2025), Arctic Wolf press releases, G2/Capterra reviews
🔵 3. DNS Market Shift — Infoblox Gains on Cisco
Cisco Secure Access threat-blocking effectiveness gap (39.3% vs. Infoblox). Market share gaining opportunity through superior threat detection narrative.
Impact: Differentiation opportunity for DNS positioning vs. Cisco
▼
Why This Matters: DNS filtering is increasingly commoditized, but threat detection effectiveness is becoming the differentiator. Cisco's slower threat intel updates vs. Infoblox's speed is eroding Cisco's traditional market share. This opens an opportunity for superior threat data positioning.
What's Happening: Q1 2026 independent testing shows Infoblox blocks 39.3% more threats than Cisco Secure Access. This gap is driven by threat intel freshness (Infoblox updates 2-4x faster). Infoblox marketing is aggressively using this data in campaigns.
Portfolio Impact:
  • 🔵 DNS Protection: We inherit BrightCloud threat intel (strong reputation). Positioning opportunity: "Superior threat detection vs. Cisco + faster updates than Infoblox".
  • 🔵 Sales: Cisco replacements are on the table. Can we win deals with "better threat blocking" narrative?
  • 🔵 Product: DNS feature parity is expected. Threat data freshness/quality is the narrative differentiator.
Recommended Action: (1) Validate BrightCloud threat detection effectiveness vs. Infoblox/Cisco. (2) Create sales narrative: "BrightCloud threat intelligence + faster blocking = lower breach risk". (3) Monitor Infoblox momentum.
Sources: Q1 2026 DNS blocking effectiveness testing (SecurityWeek), Infoblox Q1 2026 market analysis, Cisco threat intel refresh rates
