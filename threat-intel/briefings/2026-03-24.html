Top Active Threats

CVE-2026-1603 — Ivanti Endpoint Manager Auth Bypass CRITICAL · CISA KEV
Platform: Ivanti EPM (all versions < 2024 SU5) · CVSS: 8.6 · Patch available: Yes

A malformed header concatenation flaw allows unauthenticated attackers to bypass authentication and directly access the EPM Credential Vault, stealing Domain Administrator password hashes and service account credentials. No valid login required. CISA added to KEV on March 9; FCEB patch deadline was March 23.

⚡ PM Action: Ivanti EPM is widely deployed in MSP environments. Flag to MDR team for detection coverage and push patch communications to channel partners.
Sources: CybersecurityNews · CISA KEV Alert · SecurityWeek
CVE-2025-32975 — Quest KACE SMA Authentication Bypass CRITICAL · CVSS 10.0
Platform: Quest KACE Systems Management Appliance · CVSS: 10.0 · Patch available: Yes

Max-severity authentication bypass allowing attackers to impersonate legitimate users without credentials, enabling complete takeover of administrative accounts on internet-exposed KACE SMA appliances. Active exploitation observed since March 9, 2026. KACE is a common endpoint management and software deployment tool in SMB environments.

⚡ PM Action: SMBs running KACE SMA should patch immediately. EDR behavioral detections for post-exploitation activity (lateral movement, credential dumping) are critical in these environments.
Sources: The Hacker News
CVE-2026-20127 — Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Auth Bypass + Privilege Escalation CRITICAL · CVSS 10.0
Platform: Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Controller & Manager · CVSS: 10.0 · Patch available: Yes (emergency)

Authentication bypass flaw allows unauthenticated remote attackers to obtain full administrative privileges. Attackers then chain with CVE-2022-20775 to escalate to root. Actively exploited against critical infrastructure. Organizations using Cisco SD-WAN for network security should treat this as a P0 patch.

⚡ PM Action: DNS Protection positioning opportunity — network-layer attacks on SD-WAN underscore the value of DNS-layer inspection and policy enforcement as an independent security control.
Sources: Greenbone
CVE-2026-22719 — VMware Aria Operations Command Injection HIGH · CISA KEV
Platform: VMware Aria Operations · CVSS: 8.1 · Patch available: Yes

Command injection vulnerability allows an unauthenticated attacker to execute arbitrary commands on affected VMware Aria Operations instances. CISA added to KEV catalog citing active exploitation. VMware Aria is used by MSPs and enterprises for cloud and infrastructure operations management.

⚡ PM Action: MSPs running VMware environments should patch immediately. EDR detections for command execution on Aria instances should be validated.
Sources: The Hacker News · CISA
Iran-Linked MuddyWater — New Dindoor Backdoor Targeting U.S. Networks HIGH · Nation-State
Platform: Windows · Attribution: Iranian Intelligence (MOIS-linked) · Patch: N/A (behavioral)

Iran-linked MuddyWater threat group is deploying a new backdoor called Dindoor against U.S. networks. Initial access via spearphishing; Dindoor provides persistent remote access and credential harvesting capabilities. Targets include government and defense contractors but SMB supply chain exposure is a secondary risk.

⚡ PM Action: SAT — update phishing awareness training to include nation-state spearphishing lures. EDR behavioral detection for backdoor persistence mechanisms is essential.
Sources: The Hacker News

Ransomware Activity

DragonForce — MSP Supply Chain Ransomware via SimpleHelp RMM RaaS · Double Extortion
Targets: MSPs + downstream SMB clients · TTPs: RMM exploitation → lateral movement → ransomware + data theft

DragonForce ransomware group exploited a chain of three vulnerabilities in SimpleHelp RMM (CVE-2024-57726, 57727, 57728) used by a managed service provider. After gaining access to the MSP's SimpleHelp instance, attackers performed reconnaissance on downstream client environments, then deployed ransomware across multiple clients simultaneously using legitimate MSP tooling. Double extortion (encrypt + exfiltrate) was used. One victim was protected by MDR/XDR detection that caught the suspicious SimpleHelp installer push before ransomware execution.

⚡ PM Action: Strongest possible MDR case study — one client stopped, others hit. Use this as a product narrative: "MDR as the last line of defense when RMM tools become the attack vector."
Sources: BleepingComputer · MSSP Alert · Dark Reading
Anubis Ransomware — AkzoNobel Double Extortion (170GB) Double Extortion
Target: AkzoNobel (multinational chemical company) · Data claimed: ~170GB

The Anubis ransomware group published samples of allegedly stolen data from AkzoNobel, claiming to have exfiltrated roughly 170GB of internal corporate files. This is a continued demonstration of the double-extortion model where data theft is used as additional leverage alongside encryption. Anubis is an emerging group operating a RaaS model.

⚡ PM Action: Emerging RaaS group to add to threat monitoring. Reinforces backup, data loss prevention, and MDR story for SMB customers.

MSP / SMB Specific Alerts

🎯 DragonForce SimpleHelp RMM Attack — MSP Supply Chain Warning

MSPs running SimpleHelp RMM should treat CVE-2024-57726/57727/57728 as P0 patch priorities. The DragonForce supply chain attack demonstrates that attackers are now specifically targeting MSP tooling to multiply impact across an entire client base. SimpleHelp users should rotate all credentials, audit active sessions, and review push deployment logs for anomalies.

Sources: BleepingComputer · MSSP Alert
📧 Tax Season RMM Malware Campaigns — Microsoft Warning

Microsoft has warned of active phishing campaigns leveraging U.S. tax season themes (W-2s, IRS notices, tax software updates) to deliver RMM malware onto SMB endpoint environments. Attackers use legitimate-looking tax software update lures; once installed, the RMM tool provides persistent remote access. Elevated risk through April 15 tax deadline.

⚡ PM Action: Immediate SAT campaign opportunity — push tax season phishing awareness content to channel partners now. Endpoint behavioral detection for unauthorized RMM installations is key.
🇷🇺 Russian Intelligence Phishing — WhatsApp & Signal Compromise Campaigns

Threat actors affiliated with Russian Intelligence Services are conducting phishing campaigns targeting commercial messaging applications (WhatsApp, Signal) to compromise high-value individuals including government officials, journalists, and political figures. While primarily targeting high-value individuals, the TTP of messaging-app phishing is increasingly being adapted for SMB business email compromise scenarios.

⚡ PM Action: BEC and messaging-app phishing is a growing SAT topic. Ensure training modules cover multi-channel phishing, not just traditional email.

Portfolio Relevance

🛡️ Endpoint Protection & EDR

CVE-2026-1603 (Ivanti EPM) and CVE-2025-32975 (KACE) both target endpoint management infrastructure. This week's threat landscape strongly validates the need for EDR behavioral detection on systems running endpoint management tools — not just user workstations. Flag for detection engineering review.

🎯 MDR

The DragonForce/SimpleHelp supply chain attack is the strongest MDR case study in months — one MSP client stopped the attack via MDR, while others were hit. This is a direct, citable real-world example of MDR value. Prioritize for product marketing and sales enablement content.

🌐 DNS Protection

The Cisco SD-WAN and RMM malware campaigns both rely on command-and-control DNS communications for persistence. DNS-layer blocking of known C2 domains is a critical lightweight control. BrightCloud's threat intelligence feeds powering DNS protection have direct applicability here.

Tax season phishing campaigns + Russian messaging-app targeting = a two-front SAT opportunity. Push tax-themed phishing simulations now and ensure modules cover multi-channel social engineering (WhatsApp, Signal, Teams) in addition to traditional email.
